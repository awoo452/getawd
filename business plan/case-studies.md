### Beard Industries: Heroku to Amplify Static Site

### Overview
Beard Industries needed a simple marketing site without paying for a full Rails stack. The project removed unnecessary complexity, fixed contact links, and moved the site to AWS Amplify for low-maintenance hosting.

### Client
- Relationship: Paid client (introductory pricing; renewal planned for May 2026 at standard rates).
- Industry: Services/brand marketing.
- Size: Small business.
- Location: United States.
- Project type: Static marketing site.

### Problem
- A full Rails stack was being used to serve a static marketing site.

### Constraints
- Budget: Introductory pricing to keep costs low.
- Timeline: Short turnaround.
- Existing systems or tech: Rails app on Heroku.

### Goals
- Primary goal: Simplify hosting and reduce maintenance costs.
- Secondary goals: Fix broken contact flow and keep the site stable.

### Approach
- Audit the existing site and identify unnecessary app dependencies.
- Define a static-only scope and remove dynamic features.
- Plan a low-risk DNS cutover.

### Solution
- Rebuilt as a static HTML/CSS site.
- Deployed on AWS Amplify with CloudFront.
- Cleaned up contact page and links.

### Technology
- Stack: HTML/CSS.
- Hosting: AWS Amplify + CloudFront.
- Integrations: DNS for domain cutover.

### Results
- Reduced hosting complexity and ongoing maintenance.
- Eliminated Heroku overhead for a static site.
- Faster, simpler deployment workflow.

### Timeline and Budget
- Duration: Short turnaround.
- Budget range: Introductory pricing (renewal planned for May 2026).

### Lessons and Next Steps
- What worked: Right-sizing the stack to the real need.
- What would be improved: Capture load and uptime baselines pre/post migration.
- Follow-on opportunities: Optional content refresh or SEO tuning.

### Assets
- Links: beardindustries.com, github.com/awoo452/beardindustries

### Beard Bros Dumpsters: Scheduling and Operations App

### Overview
Beard Bros Dumpsters needed a straightforward customer booking flow and an internal scheduling view. A Rails app delivered reliable booking, authentication, and calendar management.

### Client
- Relationship: Family (brother).
- Industry: Dumpster rental and junk removal.
- Size: Small business.
- Location: Pennsylvania, USA.
- Project type: Custom Rails application.

### Problem
- Scheduling and booking needed to be centralized.
- The business needed a simple admin workflow.

### Constraints
- Budget: Cost-sensitive small business.
- Timeline: Staged delivery with ongoing refinements.
- Existing systems or tech: New build.

### Goals
- Primary goal: Provide a clear booking flow for customers.
- Secondary goals: Provide an internal calendar and admin controls.

### Approach
- Implement authentication and role separation.
- Build a calendar-driven scheduling interface.
- Prioritize mobile-friendly layout and clarity.

### Solution
- Rails app with Devise authentication.
- Scheduling via Simple Calendar.
- Mobile-first UI refinements and a clearer calendar legend.

### Technology
- Stack: Ruby on Rails + PostgreSQL.
- Hosting: Heroku
- Integrations: Devise, Simple Calendar.

### Results
- Booking and scheduling consolidated into one system.
- Clearer internal workflow for rentals and delivery dates.

### Timeline and Budget
- Duration: Iterative delivery.

### Lessons and Next Steps
- What worked: Calendar-first workflow and clear admin guidance.
- What would be improved: Add reporting exports for weekly volume.
- Follow-on opportunities: SEO and local lead capture optimization.

### Assets

- Links: beardbrosdumpsters.com, github.com/awoo452/beardbrosdumpsters
