# AWDevelopment Business Plan (Basic)

## Executive Summary
AWDevelopment is a solo web development studio led by Aaron, a Washington State-based developer who builds production-ready web software with an emphasis on maintainability, clarity, and predictable delivery. The company focuses on Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, and AWS, with an additional offering of fast, low-budget static or hybrid sites deployed on AWS Amplify. The immediate goal is to deliver reliable web projects for small to mid-sized businesses that need a clean startup site that can expand over time or a modernization pass on an existing site.

## Company Overview
- Business name: AWDevelopment (AWD)
- Owner: Aaron (solo operator)
- Location: Washington State, USA
- Geographic focus: US-based clients, remote-first, some ability to travel
- Core stack: Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, AWS
- Positioning: Practical, long-term maintainable software with clear delivery and stable infrastructure

## Mission and Vision
- Mission: Build practical, production-ready web software that stays simple to maintain, ships predictably, and holds up under real-world use.
- Vision: Become the trusted, low-drama development partner for businesses that value clear thinking, stable infrastructure, and systems that keep working as they grow.

## Services and Offerings
Primary offerings are shaped by demonstrated work on client and personal projects.

- Static sites on AWS Amplify (3–5+ page MVPs)
- Rails-first custom applications
- AI integration and consulting
- Internal tools and dashboards
- API integrations and automations
- Maintenance and reliability (updates, monitoring, backups)

## Target Customers
- Small to mid-sized businesses that need a low-budget startup site that can expand
- Businesses that need modernization or fixes on an existing site
- Service businesses that require online booking, scheduling, or order intake
- Teams that have an existing Rails app but need stabilization or ongoing improvements

## Value Proposition
- Practical and stable delivery over trends
- Clear reasoning, simple architecture, and minimal dependencies
- Ownership of the stack and accountability from one consistent operator
- Strong experience with full lifecycle delivery: build, deploy, maintain
- Ongoing support options to keep the codebase healthy and evolving

## Market and Competition (Basic)
The market includes general web agencies and freelancers. AWDevelopment differentiates by:

- Building for maintainability and long-term stability
- Avoiding unnecessary complexity and large dependency stacks
- Delivering with clear, predictable timelines and scope

## Market Position
AWDevelopment is positioned between low-cost site builders and high-cost agencies. The studio competes primarily with:

- Freelancers charging $75–$150/hour
- Agencies selling $8k–$40k project engagements

The positioning targets clients who need professional delivery without the overhead of a full agency team.

## Pricing Model
AWDevelopment uses fixed-scope pricing to provide predictable costs for clients and avoid hourly billing uncertainty.

Typical ranges:

- Static business websites (AWS Amplify): $1,500–$3,000
- Expanded business websites: $3,500–$7,000
- Custom Rails applications: $8,000–$25,000+
- Consulting: $125/hour (2-hour minimum)

Clients may optionally enroll in monthly hosting and maintenance packages ranging from $100–$500 per month depending on support level.
The pricing structure reflects the focus on maintainable architecture, stable infrastructure, and long-term support.

## Maintenance Packages
- Basic: $100/month (hosting, updates, backups, uptime monitoring)
- Standard: $250/month (everything in Basic, small updates, email support)
- Pro: $500/month (priority support, feature updates, performance monitoring)

## Pricing Rationale
AWDevelopment sells stability, ownership, and long-term maintainability. Pricing is framed around risk reduction and business value rather than hours.
The goal is to deliver systems that remain reliable as the business grows and avoid the typical plugin-heavy, fragile builds.

## Financial Plan (Initial)
- Short-term goal: consistent monthly project flow with low-budget retainers
- Long-term goal: stable mix of project work and retainers
- Target yearly revenue: $100k–$200k

## Risks and Mitigations
- Risk: Overreliance on a small client pool
- Mitigation: steady outbound and portfolio marketing

- Risk: Scope creep on fixed projects
- Mitigation: clear scopes, change orders, milestone billing

- Risk: Solo bandwidth limitations
- Mitigation: limit concurrent projects and enforce onboarding criteria
