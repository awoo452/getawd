# AWDevelopment Go-To-Market Plan

## Purpose
This document turns the business plan into a repeatable, measurable path to acquiring clients for AWDevelopment.

## Goals (Next 12 Months)
- Secure 1-2 monthly retainers.
- Close 2-3 fixed-scope projects per quarter.
- Publish 2-3 public case studies.
- Maintain a 2-4 week delivery lead time.

## Positioning
AWDevelopment is a low-drama, reliable development partner focused on maintainable Rails applications and fast, budget-friendly marketing sites.

### Positioning Pillars
- Practical delivery over trends.
- Clear scope and predictable timelines.
- Production-ready infrastructure from day one.

## Ideal Customer Profiles
- Local service providers (3-25 employees) with recurring scheduling, intake, or quoting needs.
- Owner-led businesses that need a reliable starter site now and a path to a custom app later.
- Teams with existing Rails apps that need stabilization, modernization, or ongoing support.

## Core Offers
- Starter Site (AWS Amplify, 1-3 key pages).
- Growth Site (Rails-based, 5-10 pages with basic workflows).
- Custom Rails App (full-stack app with PostgreSQL, staging + production).
- Stabilization Sprint (bug triage, performance, modernization).

## Sales Motion
1. Discovery call and fit check.
2. Written scope, deliverables, and assumptions.
3. Fixed price or milestone plan.
4. Weekly checkpoints during build.
5. Launch and offer ongoing maintenance.

## Channel Strategy

### Inbound
- Portfolio-first website with services, packages, and FAQs.
- SEO content around Rails modernization and small business workflows.
- Case studies highlighting outcomes and delivery clarity.

### Outbound
- Targeted outreach to local service businesses with clear workflow pain.
- Direct email or LinkedIn outreach with a short audit or teardown.
- Referrals via prior clients and local partners.

### Partnerships
- Designers who need reliable Rails delivery.
- Marketing agencies that need technical build support.
- Local tech meetups or business groups for warm introductions.

## Messaging Framework
- What we do: Production-ready Rails and AWS delivery for businesses that need stability.
- Why now: Most small businesses outgrow site builders and need a clean system.
- Why us: Clear scope, predictable delivery, long-term maintainability.

## Lead Funnel and KPIs

### Funnel Stages
1. Lead captured.
2. Discovery call booked.
3. Proposal sent.
4. Deal closed.
5. Delivery and handoff.

### Targets
- 8-12 leads per month.
- 35-50 percent discovery call booking rate.
- 30-40 percent proposal close rate.
- 1-2 retainers active by end of year.

## Required Assets
- Services page with packages and timelines.
- Pricing sheet and FAQ.
- Case study library (2-3).
- Discovery and proposal templates.
- Lead intake form with qualification questions.

### Initial Case Study Targets
- Beard Industries: Heroku to Amplify static migration.
- Beard Bros Dumpsters: Scheduling and operations app.
- Pickled Pirates Racing: Content and merch hub.

## 90-Day Plan

### Days 0-30
- Publish Go-To-Market and Case Study templates.
- Finalize services page, pricing, and FAQs.
- Set up lead intake form and qualification flow.

### Days 31-90
- Publish first case study.
- Run 2-3 outbound outreach batches per week.
- Network with 2-3 partner contacts.

### Days 91-180
- Publish second case study.
- Optimize lead intake based on conversions.
- Secure at least one retainer.

## Budget and Time Allocation
- 5-8 hours per week on outbound and content.
- 1-2 hours per week on partnerships and follow-ups.
- Minimal paid ads until case studies are live.

## Risks and Mitigations
- Risk: low inbound flow.
  - Mitigation: outbound batches and partner referrals.
- Risk: pricing mismatch with target clients.
  - Mitigation: refine package tiers and scope clarity.
- Risk: weak case study pipeline.
  - Mitigation: capture internal project outcomes and client testimonials early.

## Review Cadence
- Weekly: lead pipeline and outreach volume.
- Monthly: conversion rates and deal size.
- Quarterly: positioning and package adjustments.
